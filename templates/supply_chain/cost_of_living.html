Need help with everyday costs?

Practical local support is available for energy bills, food and housing. This page gives quick tips, where to go for help, and an easy comparison of typical rents so you can plan ahead.

  • Quick tips you can try today to save money.
  • Where to get help locally — food support, energy advice and housing services.
  • Simple rent comparison to see how areas and supermarkets compare.

Cheaper areas to rent — quick guide

Use this list as a starting point — rents change quickly, so always check current listings and transport links.

Seacroft

Affordable terraces and flats close to local shops — good for lower weekly costs.

Gipton

Lower rents for 1–2 bedroom flats and convenient bus links into the city.

Harehills

Cheaper options and shared housing (HMOs) popular with students and young workers.

Beeston

Budget-friendly area with good access to south-side employers and amenities.

Mottingham

Small pockets of cheaper housing — compare local listings carefully.

How to use this

  • Set alerts on major letting sites and compare multiple listings.
  • Visit the area at different times of day to check transport and local services.
  • Contact your local council to ask about housing support and low-cost options.

Money-saving supermarkets & tips

Small changes to shopping and meal planning can make a real difference.

  • Aldi — low prices on fresh and own-brand ranges.
  • Lidl — strong value and regular promotions.
  • Iceland — good for value frozen meals and big family packs.
  • Asda — larger value packs and budget ranges.
  • Local markets — cheaper fruit and veg on market days.

Tip: plan simple meals, buy supermarket own-brands, and cook bigger portions to freeze for later.
